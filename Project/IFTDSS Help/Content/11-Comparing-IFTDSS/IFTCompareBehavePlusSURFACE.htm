BehavePlus SURFACE Compared to IFT-surface

Fire Behavior Fuel Models

IFT-surface supports the 13 fire behavior fuel models from Anderson (1982) and the 40 additional fire behavior fuel models from Scott and Burgan (2005). IFT-surface does not support custom fuel modeling, special case fuel models (palmetto-gallberry, western aspen, or southern California chaparral), the two fuel models concept (2-D expected spread, harmonic mean, or area weighted), or exporting custom fuel model sets.

Fuel Moisture Inputs

In IFTDSS, moisture is entered by individual size classes. IFTDSS does not support moisture scenarios or the direct input of moisture by live and dead fuel categories.

Dynamic Curing Load Transfer

IFTDSS supports dynamic curing load transfer (Scott and Burgan, 2005) calculated from live herbaceous fuel moisture, but IFTDSS does not allow the transfer of live fuel load to dead fuel load to be input directly. Dynamic curing load transfer is based on the concept that the live herbaceous fuel loading is slowly transferred from the live to dead category as the fuel ages seasonally.

Wind Speed

IFTDSS supports the entry of mid-flame wind speed, but does not give the option of entering 20-ft or 10-m wind speeds with a user-supplied wind speed adjustment factor (WAF) or calculated WAF. IFTDSS always uses the maximum reliable effective wind speed (i.e., this approach cannot be disabled).

Directions

IFTDSS supports surface fire spread direction in the direction of maximum spread. In addition, you can specify that you want to receive fire behavior output for backing fire (180 degrees from the direction of maximum spread) or flanking fire (30, 45, 60, or 90 degrees from the direction of maximum spread). Wind direction is specified in degrees from north. There is no option to have winds traveling in an upslope direction.

Slope

IFTDSS supports the use of percentage as a measure of slope steepness. Percentage values are entered directly by the user; the option of using map measurements to calculate slope steepness has not been implemented.

Output Parameters

IFTDSS supports seven of the nine standard fire behavior outputs (surface rate of spread, heat per unit area, fireline intensity, flame length, reaction intensity, direction of maximum spread, and surface spread distance). The fire characteristics chart and the wind/slope/spread direction diagram are not included.

IFTDSS provides several fire behavior output variables that are calculated by the FBSDK code and the BehavePlus libraries but that are not presented in BehavePlus 5.0.5: estimates of rates of spread, surface spread distances, flame lengths, and fireline intensities for backing and flanking fires in addition to the head fire estimates provided by BehavePlus.

Other Outputs and Intermediate Values

The surface module in BehavePlus 5.0.5 has a number of optional and intermediate outputs related to adjusting wind speeds, editing and producing custom fuel models, and calculating the standard fire behavior outputs. IFTDSS does not support fuel model editing or the creation of custom fuel models so there are no output parameters for these fuel variables.
