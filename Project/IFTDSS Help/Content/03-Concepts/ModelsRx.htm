Modules for Prescribed Burn Planning

Several modules support prescribed burn planning in the areas of probability of ignition, fire behavior, fire containment, and fire effects. You can use these modules when preparing a prescribed burn plan. For example, you can use the tools in the prescribed burn planning workflow to identify under what conditions (i.e., fuel, weather, moisture, topography) to conduct a burn and to prepare your burn plan documentation. You can use the hazard analysis tools to assess the fuel characteristics and potential fire behavior in areas adjacent to a planned burn location.

Topic Module Function
Probability of Ignition Probability of ignition (IFT-ignite) The probability of ignition from either a firebrand or a lightning strike is the likelihood that either a firebrand or lightning strike will ignite ground fuel of the type specified.
Fire Behavior

Several modules in IFTDSS calculate fire behavior for various conditions.		 Crown fire behavior (IFT-crown) This module calculates the fire intensity needed for a surface fire to transition to a crown fire.
Surface fire behavior (FCCS) This module calculates surface fire behavior for selected fuelbed(s) under various environmental conditions.
Surface fire behavior (IFT-surface) This module calculates surface fire spread and intensity for individual stands.
IFT-FlamMap This module can be used to predict fire behavior for an individual stand or across a landscape. This information is useful for performing a hazard analysis and for prescribed burn planning.
Surface fire behavior, size, and spread distance A combination of the surface fire behavior and fire size and spread modules, this module can be used to calculate a surface fire's behavior, size, and growth characteristics can be calculated and used for developing a prescribed burn plan.
Fire Containment

These modules are used to calculate fire probabilities and point sources and provide estimates of needed fire suppression resources.		 IFT-contain Fire suppression resources necessary for containing a fire that is growing from a point source can be estimated.
IFT-safety The safety zone size can be calculated and used for developing a prescribed burn plan.
IFT-scorch The crown scorch height from surface fire flame lengths can be calculated.
Size and spread distance (IFT-size) A fire's behavior, size, and growth characteristics (spread) can be calculated and used for developing a prescribed burn plan.
Spotting distance (IFT-spot) Spotting distance is the maximum distance from different possible sources where one can expect potential spot fires. You can use this information when preparing a prescribed burn plan.
Fire Effects

With these modules, you can estimate a variety of fire effects across a number of fuel types. These modules make it easier for you to investigate how burning under different environmental conditions (fuel moistures) may influence how much of the fuel burns. They also provide information to use in prescribed burn planning to prepare alternative strategies to mitigate adverse fire effects, such as extensive tree mortality or excessive air emissions and smoke production. 		Consumption, emissions, and heat release (Consume) This module can be used to calculate consumption, emissions, and heat release for a variety of environmental characteristics.
IFT-FOFEM This module can be used to predict fuel consumption, emissions, tree mortality, and smoke production for individual stands.

Create Burn Plan Document

The burn plan document pathway was designed using the recommendations of the Interagency Prescribed Fire Planning and Implementation Procedures Guide (U. S. Department of Agriculture and U. S. Department of the Interior, 2008). The pathway consists of 21 elements (such as objectives and ignition plan).

You can fill in each IFTDSS page in this pathway; once it is complete, you can download the plan as a Microsoft Word document. Once the burn plan is created and downloaded, you can easily update and/or customize all sections of the burn plan using Microsoft Word.
