Crown Fire Behavior Input Parameters

Name Units Brief Description
1-h Moisture Percentage 1-hour fuel moisture content is the portion (percentage) of the 1-hour timelag dead fuel that is water, calculated on a dry weight basis.  This value is also used for dead herbaceous fuel moisture content for dynamic fuel models.
10-h Moisture Percentage The 10-hour fuel moisture content is the portion (percentage) of the 10-hour timelag dead fuel that is water, calculated on a dry weight basis.  The fuel moisture obtained for the "fuel moisture sticks" at a National Fire Danger Rating System (NFDRS) weather station is an estimate of the 10-hour fuel moisture.
20-ft Wind Speed (upslope) Miles per hour, kilometers per hour The wind speed at 20 feet above the vegetation.
100-h moisture Percentage The 100-hour fuel moisture content is the portion (percentage) of the 100-hour timelag dead fuel that is water, calculated on a dry weight basis.
Canopy Base Height Feet, meters The canopy base height (also known as crown base height) for an individual tree is the height at which sufficient fuel density exists for sustained canopy ignition. For a stand of trees, canopy base height considers both the height of the main canopy layer and the height of ladder fuels in the understory.
Canopy Bulk Density Pounds per feet3, kilograms per meter3 A stand description of weight of canopy fuels per unit of canopy volume (lb/ft3 or kg/m3).
Canopy Height Feet, meters For fire behavior modeling, canopy height or stand height is the average height of the dominant or co-dominant tree layer in a stand. Canopy height is not the height of the tallest tree in the stand or an average height of all trees within the given area. Canopy height describes the stand as it affects calculation of the wind adjustment factor in IFT-surface. If canopy height is less than 6 feet (1.8 meters), then the unsheltered WAF calculation is used.
Elapsed Time Hours The time for which the fire is spreading at an assumed constant rate.  Rate of spread is multiplied by elapsed time to calculate the spread distance of a surface fire or a crown fire.
Foliar Moisture Percentage The moisture content of the overstory foliage, the conifer needles. It is used along with fireline intensity and canopy base height to determine the threshold for transition to crown fire.
Heat per Unit Area British thermal unit per feet2, kilojoules per meter2 The heat energy release per unit area (HPUA; the unit is either square feet or square meters) within the flaming front of the surface fuel. HPUA is not affected by wind speed, slope, or direction of spread.
Live Woody Moisture Percentage Live woody fuel moisture content is the portion (percentage) of the shrub foliage and very fine stems that is water, calculated on a dry weight basis.
