Name Units Brief Description
Cover Group NA When you select a cover group in the consumption and emissions module (IFT-FOFEM), that determines the equations the module uses to calculate fire effects. These equations are based on cover types; for example, Pocosin (PC) or Ponderosa (PN). IFTDSS uses FOFEM desktop's batch mode functionality; IFTDSS does not have the direct links to the SAF/SFM, NVCS, or FCC cover classifications that are present in FOFEM desktop. In IFTDSS, you select a cover group directly based on the broad general cover groups. In FOFEM desktop, you select a SAF/SFM, NVCS, or FCC cover type, which is then mapped to a cover group. If your desired cover type does not fit into one of the cover groups provided, select “none” to use the general consumption algorithms.

Duff Depth Inches, centimeters The thickness of the duff layer laying on top of the mineral soil surface and under the litter layer.
Duff Fuel Loading Tons per acre, kilograms per meter squared The quantity of moderately to highly decomposed leaves, needles, fine twigs, and other organic material found between the mineral soil surface and litter layer on the forest floor.
Fuel Category NA The general type of fuels being burned.
  • Natural fuels are fuels that have accumulated due to natural processes, such as tree death, fallen branches, and needle cast. In general, there should be no recent management activity in natural fuels, though light thinning operations may qualify in some cases.
  • Piles are gathered and piled woody materials from management activities. They will be burned later.
  • Slash refers to areas that have experienced tree harvesting or heavy thinning operations.
Herbaceous Fuel Loading Tons per acre, kilograms per meter squared Available fuel biomass contributed by herbaceous plants; includes plants that do not develop woody, persistent tissue but are relatively soft or succulent and that sprout from the base (perennials) and plants that develop from seeds (annuals) each year. Grasses, forbs (herbaceous flowering plants), and ferns are included in this class.
Litter Fuel Loading Tons per acre, kilograms per meter squared The quantity of leaves, needles, fine twigs, and other organic material on the forest or grassland floor that have undergone little or no decomposition.
Shrub Fuel Loading Tons per acre, kilograms per meter squared Available fuel biomass contributed by woody perennial plants. Woody perennial plants differ from perennial herbs by their persistent and woody stems; and from trees by their low stature and habit of branching from the base.
