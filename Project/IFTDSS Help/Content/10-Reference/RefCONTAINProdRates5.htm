Bulldozer Fireline Construction Rates

The first table shows bulldozer fireline construction rates (single pass) in chains per hour for different bulldozer types and fire behavior fuel models. The second table defines the three bulldozer types in the first table.

   Note   >

Production rates are not precise, but instead vary with conditions. The higher rate can be used for newer bulldozers (1975 and later), bulldozers in excellent operating condition, most qualified operators, temperatures below 90°F, moist soil, few or no rocks, no lost time, indirect fireline, average fire behavior, daylight operations, and less resistive vegetative types within each fire behavior fuel model.

Minimum standards for personnel with bulldozers may differ depending on fuel type, terrain, and resource configuration. Bulldozer strike teams may use team leader in place of additional personnel per bulldozer. Two personnel per bulldozer are required for fuel that requires burnout and terrain that requires scouting.

This information is used for the Resource Line Production Rate parameter. For other fireline production rate information used for this parameter, see Sustained Fireline Production Rates, Fireline Production Rates for Initial Action by Hand Crews, Fireline Production Rates for Initial Action by Engine Crews, Fireline Explosives Crews Production Comparison, and Tractor-Plow Fireline Production Rates.
