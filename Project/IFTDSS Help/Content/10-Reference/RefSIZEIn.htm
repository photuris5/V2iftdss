Surface Fire Size Input Parameters

Name Units Brief Description
Effective Wind Speed Miles per hour, kilometers per hour The combined effect of midflame wind speed and slope steepness in the direction of maximum spread (head fire). This effective wind speed is used to determine the shape (length-to-width ratio) of a point source fire. Calculation of effective wind speed is based on the wind and slope factors that are part of Rothermel's fire spread model (1972).
Elapsed Time Hours The time during which the fire has been spreading (at an assumed constant rate). Surface Rate of Spread is multiplied by Elapsed Time to calculate the spread distance of a surface fire or a crown fire. Here, "elapsed time" is defined as the time from when a fire begins spreading (which may be different from the time of ignition) until the time that the size calculation is done.
Surface Rate of Spread (maximum) Chains per hour, meters per minute The "speed" with which the fire travels through the surface fuels. Surface fuels include the litter, grass, brush, and other dead and live vegetation within about six feet of the ground.
