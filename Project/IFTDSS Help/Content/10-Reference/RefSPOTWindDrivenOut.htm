Spotting Distance Output Parameters – Wind-Driven Surface Fires

Name Units Brief Description
Cover Height Applied for Spotting Distance from a Wind Driven Surface Fire Feet, meters The cover height actually used in the computation of spotting distance. A critical cover height is first determined from the maximum firebrand height. The applied cover height is the greater of the critical cover height and the input canopy height.
Firebrand Drift Distance from a Wind Driven Surface Fire Miles, kilometers The distance that the firebrand is carried as it is being lofted.
Firebrand Height from a Wind Driven Surface Fire Feet, meters The maximum height to which a firebrand can be lofted and still be capable of starting a fire when it lands.
Flat Terrain Spotting Distance from a Wind Driven Surface Fire Miles, kilometers The maximum spotting distance of a wind-driven surface fire over flat terrain. This value is then adjusted according to the terrain descriptors.
Spotting Distance from a Wind Driven Surface Fire Miles, kilometers The maximum distance at which one can expect potential spot fires to be started by firebrands from a spreading wind-driven surface fire. The model is applicable only if the fire is truly wind-driven through surface fuels that are not sheltered from the wind by overstory.
