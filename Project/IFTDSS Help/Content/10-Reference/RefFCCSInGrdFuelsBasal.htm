Surface Fire Behavior (FCCS) Inputs – Ground Fuels: Basal Accumulations

Basal accumulations are accumulations of bark, downed woody debris, litter, and duff surrounding tree trunks.

Name Units Brief Description
Basal accumulation type A drop-down list Defines the type of basal accumulation, which can be one of the following:
  • bark slough
  • branches
  • broadleaf evergreen
  • broadleaf deciduous
  • grass
  • needle litter
  • palm fronds
Depth Inches
 Depth of basal accumulation of ground fuels around tree trunks.

Min: minimum depth.
Mode: most common depth.
Max: maximum depth.
Percent affected Percentage
 Percentage of trees with basal accumulations of ground fuels.

Min: minimum percentage of trees affected.
Mode: most common percentage of trees affected.
Max: maximum percentage of trees affected.
Radius Feet
Radius of basal accumulation of ground fuels around tree trunks.

Min: minimum radius.
Mode: most common radius.
Max: maximum radius.
