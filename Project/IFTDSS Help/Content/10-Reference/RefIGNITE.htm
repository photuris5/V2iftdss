Probability of Ignition (IFT-ignite)

You can use IFTDSS to calculate the probability of ignition from a firebrand or a lightning strike. This probability is the likelihood that either a firebrand or lightning will ignite ground fuel of the type specified. The ignition model implemented in IFTDSS produces the same results (which may differ slightly due to rounding) as the ignition module in BehavePlus.

More information about these models (PDF)

From a Firebrand

The required input variables for ignition from a firebrand are fuel temperature and firebrand ignition. The model provides the probability of ignition from a firebrand as a percentage (Schroeder, 1969).

From Lightning

The required input variables for ignition from lightning are forest floor characteristics and fuel moisture data. The model provides the probability of ignition from a lightning strike as a percentage (Latham and Schlieter, 1989).
