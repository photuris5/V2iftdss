Containment Resources Output Parameters

Name Units Brief Description
Contain Status (A choice) The final status of the initial attack effort. This parameter has three possible values:

(1) Contained, which indicates that the fire was successfully contained using one or more of the specified resources,

(2) Withdrawn, which indicates that the fire escaped containment after resources arrived and constructed some containment line, but where the resource were withdrawn because the specified resource duration was exceeded, or

(3) Escaped, which indicates that the specified available resources are insufficient to contain the fire.
Contained Area Acres, hectares, meters squared The size of the fire enclosed by the containment line. The contained area will be larger than the fire area at the time of containment when the line construction offset is greater than zero. If the fire escapes because resource productivity is insufficient or because all specified resources have arrived at the fire and their duration times have expired, the contained area is given as -1.
Containment Diagram (forthcoming) NA When the containment diagram is implemented, it will show the final fire shape, size, and constructed fireline at the time the fire was successfully contained or escaped initial attack (all resources are applied and their durations expire before containment is achieved).
Cost of Resources Used Dollars The total cost of all resources dispatched to the fire. If a resource is dispatched to a fire, the resource cost is the resource base cost plus the resource hourly cost for the time spent on the fire until the fire is contained or the resource duration expires.
Fire Area at Initial Attack Acres, hectares, meters squared The fire size when the first resource begins suppression action on the fire. Calculated from the fire size at report and the resource arrival time.
Fireline Constructed Chains, feet, meters The total length of fireline constructed by all resources at the time the fire was contained or determined to have escaped. If the fire is not successfully contained, fireline constructed is the total length of fireline constructed by all resources at the time the last available resource duration expires. If the fire is successfully contained, fireline constructed is the total length of line constructed by all resources at the time of containment.
Number of Resources Used Whole numbers Indicates those resources actually used to contain the fire. Number of resources used may be less than the number of resources specified on the worksheet if the fire is contained before all resources arrive.
Perimeter at Initial Attack Chains, feet, meters Perimeter at initial attack is the perimeter of the elliptically shaped fire when the first resource begins suppression action on the fire. Calculated from the fire size at report and the resource arrival time.
Time from Report Hour The elapsed time from when the fire was reported until the fire was either successfully contained or escaped the initial attack made by the dispatched resources.
