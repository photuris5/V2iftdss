Performing a Landscape-Level Hazard Analysis

This PDF tutorial PDF tutorial (available in the online version of this user documentation) illustrates how to use IFTDSS to perform a landscape-level hazard analysis. The objective of a landscape-level hazard analysis is to identify areas within a landscape that could be hazardous if a fire were to occur. This analysis can help identify areas where fuels treatment may be warranted and help prioritize areas of potentially high fire hazard.

In this tutorial, a hazard analysis is performed in South Lake Tahoe’s Wildland Urban Interface to identify areas that could be hazardous if a fire were to occur.
