Exported Hourly Consumption and Emissions Parameters

When you export the hourly consumption and emissions data, they are saved in a file that uses the CSV format. The parameters in that file are described in the following table.

   Note   

The number of parameters listed in the CSV-formatted file will be much larger than shown in this table. Also, in this table, the word Pollutant represents specific pollutant names, such as CO or CH4. So, for example, you won't see "Pollutant - Flaming" in the output file, but you might see "CO - Flaming."

Name Units Brief Description
Air Temperature Fahrenheit, Celsius The ambient air temperature.
Air Temperature - Maximum Fahrenheit, Celsius The maximum ambient air temperature during a burn date.
Air Temperature - Minimum Fahrenheit, Celsius The minimum ambient air temperature during a burn day.
Briggs Buoyancy Feet4 per second3, Meters4 per second3 Same as buoyancy flux; relates to the buoyancy of the plume at the origin of the fire.
Briggs Maximum Heat Rise Feet, Meters The final effective plume height above origin of the fire based on Briggs buoyancy; models plume rise based on a smokestack model.
Briggs Stability Feet/second, Meters/second Stability coefficient.  Stability determines  whether the buoyancy of a plume increases or decreases with plume rise.
Buoyancy Efficiency NA The portion of heat release from a fire that produces buoyancy, BE.
Combustion Efficiency NA An intermediate parameter used to calculate emission rates and heat release.
Drift PM2.5 Tons, Megagrams The PM2.5 drift/smoke emissions at a given hour.
Entrainment  Efficiency NA The portion of smoke production entrained in the convection column
Lofted PM2.5 Tons, Megagrams The PM2.5 lofted/smoke emissions at a given hour.
Maximum Plume Height Feet, Meters The hourly elevation at the top of the plume using empirical equations.
Minimum Plume Height Feet, Meters The hourly elevation at the bottom of the plume using empirical equations.
Pasquil Stability NA The Pasquil stability class of the atmosphere, calculated based on midflame (surface) wind speed and solar radiation (for daytime), or surface wind speed and percent cloud cover (for nighttime). There are six Pasquil Stability categories: A through F. The Unstable classes (A, B, and C) occur during the daytime. The Stable classes (E and F) only occur at night. The Neutral class D can occur during the day or night.  Class A is ‘Very Unstable’ and corresponds to hot, calm days, which leads to the greatest amount of dispersion. A plume of smoke is broken up and spread widely with ‘A’ Stability. Class D is ‘Neutral’.  It corresponds to windy days or the transition times of dawn and dusk.  This is the most frequently occurring stability class. Class F is ‘Very Stable’ and corresponds to nights with low winds.
Plume Heat Release British thermal units, kilojoules Heat release of the plume at a given hour
Pollutant - Flaming Tons The mass of the pollutant released for each hour during the flaming combustion phase of the fire.
Pollutant - Residual Tons The mass of the pollutant released for each hour during the residual combustion phase of the fire.
Pollutant - Smoldering Tons The mass of the pollutant released for each hour during the smoldering combustion phase of the fire.
Pollutant - Total Tons The total mass of the pollutant released for each hour of the fire.
Relative Humidity Percent The relative humidity for each hour of the fire.
Relative Humidity - Maximum Percent The maximum relative humidity for a burn day.
Relative Humidity - Minimum Percent The minimum relative humidity for a burn day.
Residual Consumption Tons The cumulative amount of fuel consumed during the residual combustion phase of the fire.
Smoldering Consumption Tons The cumulative amount of fuel consumed during the smoldering combustion phase of the fire.
Smoldering Fraction Percent The percentage of total fuel consumed during the smoldering combustion phase for each hour of the fire.
Sunrise Time NA Hour of sunrise for a given burn day
Sunset Time NA Hour of sunset for a given day
Total Consumption Tons The cumulative amount of fuel consumed during the fire.
Total Flaming Tons The total mass of the pollutants consumed during the flaming combustion phase during each hour of the fire.
Total Residual Tons The total mass of the pollutants consumed during the residual combustion phase during each hour of the fire.
Total Smoldering Tons The total mass of the pollutants consumed during the smoldering combustion phase during each hour of the fire.
Transport Wind Speed Miles per hour, Kilometers per hour The wind speed within the mixing layer.  Generally refers to the rate at which emissions will be transported from one area to another.  Usually a wind under 8 mi/h (under 12.9 km/h) restricts an agency from burning.
