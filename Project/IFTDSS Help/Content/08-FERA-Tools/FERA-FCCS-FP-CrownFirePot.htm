Crown Fire Potential Output Parameters

FCCS uses a rule-based ranking of crown fire potential based on a limited scientific understanding of factors that control torching and crowning (Schaaf et al., 2007). Continuing research should provide improved quantification of the factors that control crown fire potential so that estimates of crown fire potential can be improved in future FCCS releases.

The weighted average of crown fire subpotentials are scaled to an index from 0 to 9. Crown fire potential (CFP) is calculated as follows:

CFP = (IC+TC/3+RC)/2.333

The following table describes the factors in this equation.

Name Brief Description
Crown Fire Initiation Potential (IC) The ratio of surface fireline intensity to critical fireline intensity. Represents the likelihood that a surface fire will propagate into a crown fire.
Crown Fire Spreading (Rate of Spread) Potential (RC) The relative index of crown fire spreading potential.
Crown-to-Crown Transmissivity Potential (TC) The potential for fire to carry through a forest canopy.
