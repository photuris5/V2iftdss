Input Parameters – Fuel Consumption for Activity Fuels

Name Units Brief Description

General Environmental Variables
10-hr Woody Fuel Moisture Percentage Moisture content of 10-hr (0.25 – 1 inch; 0.64 - 2.54 cm) dead fuel.
1000-hr Woody Fuel Moisture Percentage Moisture content of 1000-hour (> 3 inch; > 7.62 cm) dead fuel. Variable 1000-hr woody fuel moisture is available for this pathway. Note: If (1) snow-off occurred within the past three months, or (2) the fuelbed was harvested within the past few months, the 1000-hr woody fuels are uncured. (See Estimations for Uncured 1000-Hour Woody Fuel Moisture.) We recommend setting the 1000-hr woody fuel moisture to 60% or greater.
Duff Fuel Moisture Percentage Moisture content of duff. If your fuelbed does not have a duff layer, or you cannot measure duff fuel moisture, use the following to select the most representative fuel moisture category: Low = 30%, Medium = 70%, High = 120%.
Percent Canopy Loading Consumed Percentage Percentage of canopy loading consumed by fire. If you do not know the percent of canopy consumed, use the following guidelines: Prescribed burn = 0%, Wildfire = 60%.
Percent of Shrub Blackened by Fire Percentage Estimated percentage of the shrub stratum that is blackened by fire.

Activity Fuel Environmental Variables
1000-hr Fuel Moisture Source Drop-down list The source of the 1000-hour fuel moisture data for the unit.
  1. Measured: The directly-measured fuel moisture that represents the average unit fuel moisture of large woody fuels.
  2. NFDRS-TH: The National Fire Danger Rating System's 1000-hour fuel moisture content.
Days Since Rain Count The number of days since significant rainfall. Significant rainfall is 0.25 inch in a 48-hour period.
Length of Ignition Minutes The amount of time that it will take to ignite the area to be burned.
Midflame Wind Speed Miles/hour, kilometers/hour Midflame wind speed is the wind speed that exists at midflame height above the fuelbed and affects surface fire spread.
Slope Percentage Slope gradient.
