Surface Fire Behavior Potential Output Parameters

Surface fire behavior potential (SFP) is calculated as the maximum of flame length and rate of spread potentials, scaled to values of 0 to 9. Reaction potential is not included in the summary potential calculation. The three subcomponents of SFP are

  • Reaction potential (RP), which represents the reaction intensity (energy release per unit area per unit time) under dry fuel conditions and scaled to an index value.
  • Spread potential (SP), which is proportional to the rate of spread (distance per unit time at benchmark environmental conditions) in surface fuels.
  • Flame length potential (FP), which provides a measure of predicted flame length (in feet, at benchmark environmental conditions) proportional to fireline intensity or flame length.
Name Units Brief Description
Surface Fire Behavior Potential NA Summary surface fire behavior potential, calculated as the maximum of spread potential and flame length potential scaled to an index between 0–9.

SFP = max(RP,SP)

Where Flame length (ft; m) = Predicted flame length, measured as the distance between the flame length and the middle of the flame base.

RP = min(9,(0.08 Reaction⁢  Intensity))

Where Reaction intensity (BTU-ft2/min; kW/min) = Predicted rate of the energy release per area (square foot or square meter) within the flaming front.

Where Rate of spread (ft/min; m/min) = Predicted rate at which the flaming front passes through surface fuels.
