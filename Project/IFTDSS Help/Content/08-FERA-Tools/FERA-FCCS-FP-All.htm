All Fire and Fuel Potentials Output Parameters (FCCS)

For your convenience, this table lists all the parameters that are listed by category on these separate topic pages:

  • Available Fuel Potential Output Parameters
  • Crown Fire Potential Output Parameters
  • Surface Fire Potential Output Parameters

This table also includes the FCCS Fire Potential Code parameter.

Name Units Brief Description

FCCS Fire potential code
FCCS Fire Potential Code NA A three-digit code representing the surface fire behavior, crown fire, and available fuel potentials.

FPC = SFP, CFP, AFP

surface fire behavior potentials
Surface Fire Behavior Potential NA Summary surface fire behavior potential, calculated as the maximum of spread potential and flame length potential scaled to an index between 0–9.

SFP = max(RP,SP)
Surface Fire Flame Length Potential NA Proportional to fireline intensity or flame length.

FP = min(9,(2 Flame⁢ Length))

Where Flame length (ft; m) = Predicted flame length, measured as the distance between the flame length and the middle of the flame base.
Surface Fire Reaction Potential NA Index of reaction intensity (energy released per unit area and time)

RP = min(9,(0.08 Reaction⁢  Intensity))

Where Reaction intensity (BTU-ft2/min;
kW/min) = Predicted rate of the energy release per area (square foot or square meter) within the flaming front.
Surface Fire Spread Potential NA Proportional to the no-wind rate of spread in surface fuel (distance per unit time). 

SP = min(9,(2 Rate⁢ of⁢ Spread))

Where Rate of spread (ft/min; m/min) = Predicted rate at which the flaming front passes through surface fuels.

Crown fire potentials
Crown Fire Initiation Potential NA The potential for fire to reach the canopy layer.
Crown Fire Potential NA The weighted average of crown fire subpotentials scaled to an index between 0–9.

CFP = (IC+TC/3+RC)/2.333
Crown Fire Spreading Potential NA The relative index of crown fire rate of spread.
Crown-to-Crown Transmissivity Potential NA The potential for fire to carry through a canopy.

available fuel potentials
Available Fuel Potential NA The sum of fuel loadings in all combustion phases scaled to an index between 0–9.

AFP = min(9, sum(FA,SA,RA))
Flame Available Fuel Potential 10 tons/acre The sum of fuel loadings available for the flaming phase of combustion.

FA = FA/10
Residual Available Fuel Potential 10 tons/acre The sum of fuel loadings available for the residual smoldering phase of combustion.

RA = RA/10
Smoldering Available Fuel Potential 10 tons/acre The sum of fuel loadings available for the smoldering phase of combustion.

SA = SA/10
