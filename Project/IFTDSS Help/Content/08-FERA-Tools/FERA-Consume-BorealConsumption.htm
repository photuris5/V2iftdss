Boreal Forest Floor Consumption

The Alaska forest floor (Boreal forest floor) consumption study opportunistically measured pre- and post-burn forest floor loading in black and white spruce forests burned by wildfires in Alaska (Ottmar and Sandberg, 2003; Ottmar and Baker, 2007).  During the 2003 fire season, field efforts were concentrated on three wildfires:

  • Erickson Creek wildfire along the Dalton Highway fifty miles northwest of Fairbanks
  • Chena Lakes wildfire 10 miles east of Fairbanks
  • Black Hills wildfire, 50 miles east of Tok, Alaska

During the 2004 fire season, five wildfires were sampled, including the Chicken, Porcupine, Wall Street, Kings Creek, and Gardiner wildfires 50 to 100 miles north and east of Tok.

Boreal Forest Floor Consumption Equation

Western woody fuel equations are used to represent boreal downed wood consumption.  The forest floor equation represented here is suitable for deep forest floor layers in Alaska or other boreal forests.
