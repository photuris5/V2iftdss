Element 7 - Environmental and Fire Behavior Prescription

Prescription is defined as “the measurable criteria that define a range of conditions during which a prescribed fire may be ignited and held as a prescribed fire.”

The burn plan environmental prescription will describe:

  • a range of low to high limits for the environmental (weather, topography, fuels, etc.) parameters
  • a set of maximum fire behavior parameters in case of escape
    • hottest, driest, windiest prescription limits
    • Most extreme environmental conditions (slope, aspect)

(USDA and U.S. DOI, 2014, p. 28)

Once you navigate to Element 7: Prescription, fill in the Environmental Prescription, including environmental parameters, inputs, and outputs you have determined from Behave.

   Note   

You can wait and populate the prescription once you finish the burn plan and export it to Word.

You have no available data sets to import data from because you have not run any fire behavior modules within IFTDSS. Once you run fire behavior in one of the IFTDSS Prescribed Burn Planning Workflow pathways, you will have outputs to import into Element 7.

IFTDSS contains an 'Import Data' feature. While not used in the exercise, it allows for the easy import of prescription data from other runs conducted in IFTDSS. It is described in more detail under Tutorial C of the IFTDSS online help.

   Note   

Burning when all environmental prescription variables are at or near the prescription extremes will likely exceed desired prescribed fire behavior characterisitcs, and should be considered out of the range of conditions that meet the prescription (USDA and U.S. DOI 2014 p. 28)
