Hazard Workflow

The hazard analysis workflow provides tools to perform a current condition assessment of fire hazard within an area of interest. This workflow identifies areas that warrant further analysis based on potential fire hazard.

The Hazard Analysis workflow provides for evaluation of fire behavior and fire effects. The fire behavior modules run FlamMap, minimum travel time (MTT), and random ignition (RANDIG) to model fire behavior across a landscape using LANDFIRE (.lcp) data. They include:

Calculate burn probability across a landscape (using IFT-RANDIG)

Calculate burn probability across a landscape (using IFT-RANDIG)

RANDIG is a mechanistic fire spread program that uses the minimum travel time algorithms in FlamMap to simulate fire spread across landscapes using constant wind and fuel moisture inputs (Finney, 2006). RANDIG creates raster maps of composite burn probabilities (burn probabilities for all flame lengths) and raster maps of burn probabilities for a set of user-defined flame length classes.

Inputs

Outputs

Name Units Brief Description
Burn Probability at Low Flame Lengths Percentage The likelihood that if the area represented by a pixel burns, it will burn within the user-defined low flame length class.
Burn Probability at Medium Flame Lengths Percentage The likelihood that if the area represented by a pixel burns, it will burn within the user-defined medium flame length class.
Burn Probability at High Flame Lengths Percentage The likelihood that if the area represented by a pixel burns, it will burn within the user-defined high flame length class.
Burn Probability at Very High Flame Lengths Percentage The likelihood that if the area represented by a pixel burns, it will burn within the user-defined very high flame length class.
Overall Burn Probability Percentage The likelihood that the area represented by a pixel will burn given a random ignition across the area of interest (for a specific set of environmental conditions). Burn probability is not an estimate of the future likelihood of a wildfire and should not be confused with empirical wildfire occurrence probabilities.

Calculate fire behavior across a landscape (using IFT-FlamMap)

Calculate fire behavior across a landscape (using IFT-FlamMap)

With IFT-FlamMap, IFTDSS provides tools for calculating potential fire behavior using surface fire behavior. The spatial fire behavior outputs provide information about the distribution of possible fire behaviors across an area of interest. This information, when coupled with estimates of tree mortality, fuels consumption, soil heating, and emissions (from fire effects models), provides the fuels treatment specialist with a range of spatial data to use in assessing potential fire hazard across a landscape.

Inputs

Name Units Brief Description

Fuel Moisture
1-hr Fuel Moisture Percentage Moisture content of 1-hour fuel (dead woody fuel 0 to ¼ inch or 0 to 6.4 mm in diameter) is the mass of water within the fuel particle expressed as a percentage of the fuel particle’s oven-dry mass.
10-hr Fuel Moisture Percentage Moisture content of 10-hour fuel (dead woody fuel ¼ to 1 inch or 0.6 to 2.5 cm in diameter) is the mass of water within the fuel particle expressed as a percentage of the fuel particle’s oven-dry mass.
100-hr Fuel Moisture Percentage Moisture content of 100-hour fuel (dead woody fuel 1 to 3 inches or 2.5 to 7.6 cm in diameter) is the mass of water within the fuel particle expressed as a percentage of the fuel particle’s oven-dry mass.
Live Herbaceous Fuel Moisture Percentage Moisture content of live herbaceous fuel is the mass of water within the fuel particle expressed as a percentage of the fuel particle’s oven-dry mass.
Live Woody Fuel Moisture Percentage Moisture content of live woody fuel is the mass of water within the fuel particle expressed as a percentage of the fuel particle’s oven-dry mass.

Weather
20-ft Wind Speed Miles per hour, kilometers per hour Unless otherwise specified, the wind speed is the wind velocity measured 20 ft (6 m) above ground.
Wind Direction Degrees Wind direction is the direction from which the wind is blowing, specified in degrees increasing clockwise from north (which is zero degrees).

Outputs

Crown Fire Activity NA A surface fire may make the transition to some form of crown fire depending on surface fire intensity and crown characteristics. The crown characteristics used to compute crown fire activity include canopy base height, stand height, canopy bulk density, and foliar moisture content.
Direction of Maximum Spread Degrees The heading direction of a fire. Direction of maximum spread results from the algebraic combination of the wind vector and slope vector.
Fireline Intensity Btus/foot/second The rate of energy or heat release per unit length of fire front, regardless of its depth. In other words, the energy released at the flaming front of an actively burning fire.
Flame Length Feet, meters The distance from the ground at the leading edge of the flame to the tip of the flame.
Heat per Unit Area Btus/ft2 Total amount of heat released per unit area as the flaming front of the fire passes.
Horizontal Movement Rate Chains per hour, meters per minute The rate of spread (slope basis) transformed to a horizontal projection.
Mid-flame Wind Speed Miles per hour, kilometers per hour Wind speed at a height above the ground equal to one-half of the flame height. However, mid-flame wind speeds are commonly referred to as eye level winds.
Rate of Spread Chains per hour, meters per minute The linear rate of advance of a fire front in the direction perpendicular to the fire front.

Calculate minimum travel time (using MTT)

Calculate minimum travel time (using MTT)

The minimum travel time (MTT) module is a two-dimensional fire growth model. Fire growth and behavior are calculated by searching for pathways with minimum fire spread times from point, line, or polygon ignition sources.

Inputs

Name Units Brief Description

Fuel Moisture
1-hr Fuel Moisture Percentage Moisture content of 1-hour fuel (dead woody fuel 0 to 1/4 inch or 0 to 6.4 mm in diameter) is the mass of water within the fuel particle expressed as a percentage of the fuel particle’s oven-dry mass.
10-hr Fuel Moisture Percentage Moisture content of 10-hour fuel (dead woody fuel 1/4 to 1 inch or 0.6 to 2.5 cm in diameter) is the mass of water within the fuel particle expressed as a percentage of the fuel particle’s oven-dry mass.
100-hr Fuel Moisture Percentage Moisture content of 100-hour fuel (dead woody fuel 1 to 3 inches or 2.5 to 7.6 cm in diameter) is the mass of water within the fuel particle expressed as a percentage of the fuel particle’s oven-dry mass.
Live Herbaceous Fuel Moisture Percentage Moisture content of live herbaceous fuel is the mass of water within the fuel particle expressed as a percentage of the fuel particle’s oven-dry mass.
Live Woody Fuel Moisture Percentage Moisture content of live woody fuel is the mass of water within the fuel particle expressed as a percentage of the fuel particle’s oven-dry mass.

Weather
20-ft Wind Speed Miles per hour, Kilometers per hour Unless otherwise specified, the wind speed is the wind velocity measured 20 ft (6 m) above ground.
Wind Direction Degrees Wind direction is the direction from which the wind is blowing, specified in degrees increasing clockwise from north (which is zero degrees).

Simulation Inputs
Duration of the Simulation Minutes The duration of the fire growth calculations for the set of constant wind and fuel moisture conditions. Range: 1 to 720 min.
Fill Barriers Y/N Barriers can be points, lines or polygons depending on the pattern of mouse button clicks used and either filled or unfilled. Only closed polygons can be filled with an unburnable interior. (Yes or No).
Spot Fire Probability NA The probability of spot fires. Spot probability values must be between 0 and 1, where 1 equals 100% and 0 will turn spotting off. Embers are produced only with passive and active crown fire. The spotting tree species is always grand fir (Abies grandis), and the diameter at breast height is always 7.9 inches (20 cm). Range: 0.00 to 1.00.
Travel Path Interval Feet, Meters Controls the major flow paths at a specified distance interval. Range: 1 to 5,000 meters or 3 to 16,404 feet.

Outputs

Name Units Brief Description
All flow paths NA This theme displays the minimum time pathways between all nodes on the analysis area.
Crown Fire Activity NA A surface fire may make the transition to some form of crown fire depending on surface fire intensity and crown characteristics. The crown characteristics used to compute crown fire activity include canopy base height, stand height, canopy bulk density, and foliar moisture content.
Fire arrival time Fraction of an hour A representation of the time (as the fraction of an hour, 0.00 to 1.00) it takes the fire to reach that location on the landscape from the ignition source.
Fireline Intensity Btus/foot/second, kW/meter The rate of energy or heat release per unit length of fire front, regardless of its depth, if the fire was spreading in the direction of maximum spread rate. In other words, the energy released at the flaming front of an actively burning fire.
Flame Length Feet, meters The distance from the ground at the leading edge of the flame to the tip of the flame.
Major flow paths NA This is a subset of the MTT Flow Paths showing the most significant fire spread pathways. The spacing is determined by the Travel Path Interval (distance) setting.
Rate of Spread Chains per hour, meters per minute The linear rate of advance of a fire front in the direction perpendicular to the fire front, if the fire was spreading in the direction of maximum spread rate.
Rate of Spread (MTT) Chains per hour, meters per minute The linear rate of advance of a fire front in the direction perpendicular to the fire front, for the spread direction of the simulated fire.

For more information on using IFT Minimum Travel Time visit the IFT-MTT reference page.
