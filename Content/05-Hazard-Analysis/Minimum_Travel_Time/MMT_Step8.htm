Inputs

In the Inputs window we will set the environmental conditions and duration for the run. Under properties, we can specify the crown fire calculation method as either Finney or Scott and Reinhardt.

The conditions for the wildfire can be determined in a number of ways. You might use FireFamilyPlus to determine the 90th, 95th, or 97th percentile weather or you may choose to use red-flag conditions depending on the purpose of analysis. In this tutorial we are assuming conditions typical in August.

We will use a hypothetical scenario with 12 hours (720 minutes) of burning or nearly 2 full burn periods. You may set your own conditions or use the below conditions for your run:

  • 1 hr fuel moisture: 4
  • 10 hr fuel moisture: 6
  • 100 hr fuel moisture: 8
  • Live herbaceous fuel moisture: 30
  • Live woody fuel moisture: 90
  • Wind direction: 200
  • 20-foot windspeed: 16

Travel path return intervals will be displayed at 500 ft intervals and spot fire probability will be left at 0%. Ignitions and barriers will be created in the next two steps. Click Next.
