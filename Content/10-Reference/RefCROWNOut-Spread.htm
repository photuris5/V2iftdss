Crown Fire Behavior Spread Output Parameters

Name Units Brief Description
Active Crown Fire Yes or No Either Yes or No. This parameter indicates whether conditions are such that a fire can maintain itself as an active crown fire. If the active ratio is greater than or equal to 1, then Active Crown Fire is Yes. If the active ratio is less than 1, then Active Crown Fire is No.
Active Ratio A ratio The crown fire rate of spread divided by the critical crown fire rate of spread. If the active ratio is greater than or equal to 1, then the fire may be an active crown fire (Van Wagner, 1989; Van Wagner, 1993; Finney, 1998; Scott and Reinhardt, 2001).
Critical Crown Rate of Spread Chains per hour, meters per minute The rate at which a crown fire must spread to maintain itself as an active crown fire (Van Wagner, 1977). Critical crown rate of spread is a function of the canopy bulk density, from which it is calculated.
Critical Surface Flame Length Feet, meters Associated with the critical surface intensity required for transition to crown fire.
Critical Surface Intensity Btus per feet per second, kilowatts per meter The fireline intensity of the surface fire required for a transition from a surface fire to a crown fire.  Critical surface intensity is calculated from foliar moisture content and canopy base height.
Crown Fire Area Acres, hectares Assumes an elliptically shaped fire. The model is in Rothermel (1991, p.16).
Crown Fire Length-to-Width Ratio A ratio Based on 20-foot wind speed as described by Rothermel (1991, p.16).
Crown Fire Perimeter Chains, meters Assumes an elliptically shaped fire. The model is in Rothermel (1991, p.16).
Crown Rate of Spread Chains per hour, meters per minute The forward spread rate of a crown fire estimated using Rothermel's (1991) crown fire spread model. Crown fire rate of spread is the overall spread for a sustained run over several hours. The spread rate includes the effects of spotting and is calculated from 20-ft wind speed and surface fuel moisture values. Crown fire rate of spread does not consider a description of the overstory.
Crown Spread Distance Chains, meters The forward spread distance of a crown fire calculated from crown rate of spread and elapsed time. Crown fire spread distance is the overall spread distance for a sustained run over several hours. The spread distance includes the effects of spotting.
Fire Type Drop-down choice of fire type One of the following four fire types:
  • Surface (understory fire)
  • Torching (passive crown fire; surface fire with occasional torching trees)
  • Conditional crown (active crown fire possible if the fire transitions to the overstory)
  • Crowning (active crown fire; fire spreading through the overstory crowns)

(Van Wagner, 1993; Finney, 1998; Scott and Reinhardt, 2001)
Transition Ratio A ratio The surface fireline intensity divided by the critical surface intensity.  If the transition ratio is greater than or equal to 1, then the surface fireline intensity is sufficient for a transition to crown fire (Van Wagner, 1989; Van Wagner, 1993; Finney, 1998; Scott and Reinhardt, 2001).
Transition to Crown Fire Yes or No Either Yes or No. This parameter indicates whether the fire is likely to transition from being a surface fire to being a crown fire. The calculation depends on the transition ratio.

If the transition ratio is greater than or equal to 1, then it is likely that the fire will transition to a crown fire (Yes).

If the transition ratio is less than 1, then it isn't likely that the fire will transition to crown fire (No).
