IFT-FVS – Prescribed Burn Input Parameters

Parameter Unit Description
Treatment Year Year Schedules a thinning from below to a basal area per acre target, or a trees per acre target. Only trees with dbh’s and heights within the user-defined ranges are considered for removal.
20-ft Wind Speed Miles per hour The wind velocity measured 20 ft (6 m) above ground. Range: 0 to 100.
Moisture Drop-down list Select a moisture option from the list. Options: Very dry; Dry (default); Moist; Wet.
Air Temperature Fahrenheit The ambient dry bulb temperature measured in the shade. This value is used to calculate scorch height and probability of ignition from a firebrand. Range: -40 to 120.
Tree Mortality Drop-down list The mortality code used in the prescribed fire treatment calculations. Choose whether to turn off the fire and fuels extension (FFE) mortality predictions, or use FFE to estimate tree mortality (Ryan and Reinhardt 1988). Options: Turn off FEE mortality (0); FEE estimates mortality (1, default).
Stand Area Burned Percent The percentage of the stand area burned in the prescribed fire treatment.
Season of the Fire Drop-down list The season the prescribed fire treatment takes place. Options: Early spring (compact leaves); Before greenup; After greenup (before fall); Fall.

Moisture

Parameter Unit Description
1-hr Woody Fuel Moisture Percent Moisture content of 1-hour fuel (dead woody fuel 0 to ¼ inch or 0 to 6.4 mm in diameter) is the mass of water within the fuel particle expressed as a percentage of the fuel particle’s oven-dry mass. Range: 1 to 60.
10-hr Woody Fuel Moisture Percent Moisture content of 10-hour fuel (dead woody fuel ¼ to 1 inch or 0.6 to 2.5 cm in diameter) is the mass of water within the fuel particle expressed as a percentage of the fuel particle’s oven-dry mass. Range: 1 to 60.
100-hr Woody Fuel Moisture Percent Moisture content of 100-hour fuel (dead woody fuel 1 to 3 inches or 2.5 to 7.6 cm in diameter) is the mass of water within the fuel particle expressed as a percentage of the fuel particle’s oven-dry mass. Range: 1 to 60.
1000-hr Woody Fuel Moisture Percent Moisture content of 1000-hour fuel (dead woody fuel >3 inches or >7.6 cm in diameter) is the mass of water within the fuel particle expressed as a percentage of the fuel particle’s oven-dry mass. Range: 1 to 300.
Live Woody Fuel Moisture Percent Moisture content of live woody fuel is the mass of water within the fuel particle expressed as a percentage of the fuel particle’s oven-dry mass. Range: 30 to 300.
Live Herbaceous Fuel Moisture Percent Moisture content of live herbaceous fuel is the mass of water within the fuel particle expressed as a percentage of the fuel particle’s oven-dry mass. Range: 30 to 300.
Duff Fuel Moisture Percent Moisture content of duff is the mass of water within the duff layer expressed as a percentage of the duff's oven-dry mass. Range: 1 to 700.
