Surface Fire Behavior Outputs – Western Aspen

You can select one or more of these options.

Name Units Brief Description
1-hour fuel load Tons per acre, tonne per hectare (Mg/ha) The weight of the 1-hour dead fuel per unit area. This value is a fuel model parameter. The 1-hour timelag dead fuel category includes dead fuel from 0 to 0.25 inches (0-0.64 cm) in diameter. This category includes needles, leaves, cured herbaceous plants, and fine dead stems of plants.
1-hour SA/V Feet2 per feet3, meter2 per meter3 1-hour SA/V ratio is the amount of area on the outside of the fuel (surface area) divided by the volume of the fuel. 1-hour fuel is dead fuel 0 to 0.25 inches (0 to 0.64 cm) in diameter. The 1-hour SA/V is a fuel model parameter.
Live herbaceous fuel load Tons per acre, tonne per hectare (Mg/ha) The weight of live herbaceous fuel per unit of area. This value is a fuel model parameter. Live herbaceous fuels are living grasses and forbs, either annual or perennial. If the fuel model is dynamic, the initial live herbaceous load is stored with the fuel model. A portion of the live herbaceous fuel load is transferred to dead herbaceous fuel load. The portion of the load transferred is either specified by the user or calculated from live herbaceous moisture.
Live woody fuel load Tons per acre, tonne per hectare (Mg/ha) The weight of live woody fuel per unit area. This value is a fuel model parameter. Live woody fuels include the foliage and very fine stems of living shrubs.
Live woody SA/V Feet2 per feet3, meter2 per meter3 Live woody SA/V ratio is the total surface area of a shrub plant leaf divided by the volume occupied by the plant leaf. This value is a fuel model parameter.
Probability of aspen mortality Percentage The likelihood that a tree will be killed by a fire. Probability of aspen mortality is based on flame length, D.B.H., and fire severity (either low or moderate+). Low fire severity refers to fires that char but do not completely consume leaf litter, and that have patches of unburned vegetation and litter. Moderate+ fire severity refers to fires that consume litter and some duff. (Brown and Debyle, 1987.)
