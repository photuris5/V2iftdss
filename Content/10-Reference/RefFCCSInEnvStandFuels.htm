Surface Fire Behavior (FCCS) Inputs – Environmental Parameters: Standard Fuel Moisture Scenarios

The Standard Fuel Moisture tab lists 16 fuel moisture scenarios from BehavePlus.

  • D1L1 – Very low dead, fully cured herb
  • D1L2 – Very low dead, 2/3 cured herb
  • D1L3 – Very low dead, 1/3 cured herb
  • D1L4 – Very low dead, fully green herb
  • D2L1 – Low dead, fully cured herb
  • D2L2 – Low dead, 2/3 cured herb
  • D2L3 – Low dead, 1/3 cured herb
  • D2L4 – Low dead, fully green herb
  • D3L1 – Moderate dead, fully cured herb
  • D3L2 – Moderate dead, 2/3 cured herb
  • D3L3 – Moderate dead, 1/3 cured herb
  • D3L4 – Moderate dead, fully green herb
  • D4L1 – High dead, fully cured herb
  • D4L2 – High dead, 2/3 cured herb
  • D4L3 – High dead, 1/3 cured herb
  • D4L4 – High dead, fully green herb

When you select one of these fuel moisture scenarios, IFTDSS assigns moisture values (using each of the parameters from the following table) to that scenario.

Name Units Brief Description
1-hour Percentage 1-hour fuel moisture (0.0 to 0.25 inch diameter woody fuels).
10-hour Percentage 10-hour fuel moisture (0.25 to 1.0 inch diameter woody fuels).
100-hour Percentage 100-hour fuel moisture (1.0 to 3.0 inch diameter woody fuels).
1000-hour Percentage 1000-hour fuel moisture (3.0 to 9.0 inch diameter woody fuels).
Crown foliar moisture Percentage Live foliar moisture of tree crowns.
Duff fuel moisture Percentage Duff fuel moisture.
Nonwoody Percentage Live foliar moisture of nonwoody fuels.
Shrub Percentage Live foliar moisture of shrubs.
