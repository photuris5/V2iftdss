Surface Fire Behavior Input Options – Directions

Name Units Brief Description
Surface fire spread direction NA You have two options:
  • is only in the direction of maximum spread.

    When you choose this option, the spread rate is calculated only in the direction of maximum spread. Surface fire spread direction is determined by slope and wind direction.
  • is in directions specified on the worksheet.

    When you choose this option, the spread rate is calculated for the directions specified on the worksheet.
Wind Degrees You have two options:
  • The wind direction is specified on the worksheet.
  • is upslope.

    When you choose this option, the wind is assumed to be blowing upslope, so wind direction is not required as an input. This is a simple, frequently used case. The head fire is upslope with the wind.
Wind and spread directions Degrees Surface spread direction is the direction toward which the surface fire is calculated to be going. Spread direction can be specified as degrees clockwise from upslope (the direction the wind is blowing) or from the north, depending on the selected input option. You have two options:
  • are degrees clockwise from upslope (direction the wind is pushing the fire).

    When you choose this option, you are specifying the direction in which the wind is pushing the fire as measured in degrees clockwise from upslope. This choice is useful in examining the vectoring approach to wind and slope influences.
  • are degrees clockwise from north (the direction from which the wind is blowing).

    When you choose this option, you are specifying the direction in which the wind is pushing the fire as measured in degrees clockwise from north.
