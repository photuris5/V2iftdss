Surface Fire Behavior (FCCS) Inputs – Ground Fuels: Duff

Duff comprises the partially to fully decomposed organic material between the LLM stratum and mineral soil. Duff is described in two layers: an upper (fermentation) layer and a lower (humus) layer.

Name Units Brief Description
Depth Inches
 Depth of upper or lower duff layer.

Min: minimum depth.
Mode: most common depth.
Max: maximum depth.
Derivation A drop-down list Derivation of the upper and lower duff layers. The choices are
  • Upper duff: The fermentation layer, comprising partially decomposed dead moss and litter and/or partially decomposed sphagnum moss or sedge.
  • Lower duff: The humus layer, comprising fully decomposed moss and litter and/or fully decomposed sphagnum moss or sedge.
Percent cover Percentage Percentage cover of the upper or lower duff layer.

Min: minimum percentage cover.
Mode: most common percentage cover.
Max: maximum percentage cover.
Percent rotten Percentage Percentage of rotten wood in both duff layers.

Min: minimum percentage of rotten wood.
Mode: most common percentage of rotten wood.
Max: maximum percentage of rotten wood.
