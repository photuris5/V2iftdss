Fire Behavior (IFT-FlamMap)

FlamMap is a fire behavior mapping and analysis software application that computes potential fire behavior characteristics (such as spread rate, flame length, and fireline intensity) over an entire landscape under constant weather and fuel moisture conditions.

In IFTDSS's IFT-FlamMap, we have implemented the basic FlamMap fire behavior modeling features to predict fire behavior for an individual stand or across a landscape. Basic FlamMap simulates surface and crown fire behavior characteristics using Rothermel’s 1972 surface fire model, Van Wagner’s 1977 crown fire initiation model, and Rothermel’s 1991 crown fire spread model for a single instance in time. In IFTDSS, this module can be used for performing a hazard analysis and for prescribed burn planning.

Individual Stands

The individual stands module computes potential fire behavior characteristics at the stand level.

  • Input variables include a fuel model, site information, canopy characteristics, moisture, and wind parameters.
  • Output variables include fire behavior parameters, such as flame length, rate of spread, and fireline intensity.

Across a Landscape (Spatial Module)

The spatial module computes potential fire behavior characteristics at a landscape level. You can upload a spatial data set (i.e., LANDFIRE .lcp data) or define the spatial extent manually.

  • Input variables include environmental (moisture and wind) characteristics.
  • Output variables include various fire behavior parameters, such as flame length, rate of spread, and fireline intensity.
