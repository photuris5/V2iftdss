Name Units Brief Description
Fireline Intensity Btus per feet per second,
kilowatts per meter,
joules per meter per second		 The heat energy released per unit of time from a one-foot-wide (or one-meter-wide) section of the fuel bed extending from the front to the rear of the flaming front. Fireline intensity is a function of rate of spread and heat per unit area, and is directly related to flame length.
Flame Length Feet, meters The flame length of a spreading surface fire within the flaming front is measured from midway in the active flaming combustion zone to the average tip of the flames.

For a visual definition of flame length, see Utah State University's The Changing Fire Environment.
