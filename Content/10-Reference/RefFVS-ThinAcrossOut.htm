IFT-FVS – Thin A Species Across a DBH Range – Output Parameters

Stand Characteristics

Parameter Unit Description
Forest Type NA The type of forest that the stand represents.
Size Class NA A size class category that best represents the stand. Classified as sawtimber, poletimber, seedling-sapling, chaparral, or nonstocked.
Stocking Class NA A stocking class that best represents the stand. Classified as overstocked, fully stocked, moderately stocked, poorly stocked, or nonstocked.
Stand Age Years The age of the overstory.
Tree Density Trees per acre The density of the stand.
Trees Removed Trees per acre The amount of trees removed during the treatment
Quadratic Mean DBH Inches The diameter at breast height (4.5 ft; 1.37 m) of the tree of average basal area.
Quadratic Mean DBH (post) Inches The diameter at breast height (4.5 ft; 1.37 m) of the tree of average basal area post-treatment.
Basal Area Feet2 per acre The cross sectional area of a stand, measured at breast height.
Basal Area (post) Feet2 per acre The cross sectional area of a stand, measured at breast height post treatment.
Stand Density Index NA An expression of relative stand density based on the predictable relationship between average tree size and trees per unit area in dense stands. This relationship, independent of both stand age and site quality, provides an understanding of the competitive interactions between individuals in a population.
Stand Density Index (post) NA An expression of relative stand density based on the predictable relationship between average tree size and trees per unit area post-treatment. This relationship, independent of both stand age and site quality, provides an understanding of the competitive interactions between individuals in a population.
Top Height Feet The current, average height of the dominant trees, computed by averaging the heights of the 40 largest diameter trees/ac (99 trees/ha).
Top Height (post) Feet The current, average height of the dominant trees, computed by averaging the heights of the 40 largest diameter trees/ac (99 trees/ha) post-treatment.
Canopy Base Height Feet The height of the base of the canopy.
Canopy Base Height (post) Feet The height of the base of the canopy post-treatment.
Canopy Bulk Density Kilograms per meter3 The bulk density of the canopy.
Canopy Bulk Density (post) Kilograms per meter3 The bulk density of the canopy post-treatment.
Canopy Cover Percent The percent of a stand covered by the crowns of live trees.
Canopy Cover (post) Percent The percent of a stand covered by the crowns of live trees post-treatment.
Crown Competition Factor Percent A relative measure of stand density that represents the percent area covered by the vertical projections of tree crowns (Krajicek and others 1961), and is based on the potential crown area expected for each tree under optimal growing conditions. Stand crown competition factor is the summation of individual tree values. A value of 100% theoretically indicates that tree crowns will just touch in an unthinned, evenly spaced stand.
Crown Competition Factor (post) Percent A relative measure of stand density (post-treatment) that represents the percent area covered by the vertical projections of tree crowns (Krajicek and others 1961), and is based on the potential crown area expected for each tree under optimal growning conditions. Stand crown competition factor is the summation of individual tree values. A value of 100% theoretically indicates that tree crowns will just touch in an unthinned, evenly spaced stand.
Total Volume Feet3 per acre The total volume of trees in a given area.
Total Volume Removed Feet3 per acre The total volume of trees in a given area removed during treatment.
Merchantable Volume Feet3 per acre The merchantable volume of trees in a given area.
Merchantable Volume Removed Feet3 per acre The merchantable volume of trees in a given area removed during treatment.
Accretion Feet3/acre/year The annual growth on all trees that were measured at the beginning of a defined period of growth.
Mortality Feet3/acre/year The annual mortality of trees in a given area, over a defined period of time.

Fuel Loading

Parameter Unit Description
Total Fuel Loading Tons per acre Total biomass of all standing wood and surface fuels.
Small Diameter Branches and Stemwood Tons per acre Biomass of live, small diameter branches and stemwood (0 to 3 in; 0 to 7.62 cm).
Large Diameter Branches and Stemwood Tons per acre Biomass of live, large diameter branches and stemwood (>3 in; >7.62 cm).
Live Foliage Tons per acre Biomass of foliage from live trees.
Small Diameter Branches and Snags Tons per acre Biomass of dead standing wood consisting of small diameter branches and snags (0 to 3 in; 0 to 7.62 cm).
Large Diameter Branches and Snags Tons per acre Biomass of dead standing wood consisting of large diameter branches and snags (>3 in; >7.62 cm).
Total Surface Fuel Tons per acre Total biomass of all surface fuel (live shrubs, live herbaceous, litter, duff, and downed woody fuel).
Live Shrub Tons per acre Biomass of the live shrub layer.
Live Herbaceous Tons per acre Biomass of the live herbaeous layer.
Fine Woody Fuel Tons per acre Biomass of all fine woody fuel (0 to 3 in; 0 to 7.62 cm).
Total 1000-hr Woody Fuel Tons per acre Total biomass of 1000-hr woody fuel (>3 in; >7.62 cm).
Small Diameter 1000-hr Woody Fuel Tons per acre Biomass of small diameter 1000-hr woody fuel (3 to 6 in; 7.62 to 15.24 cm).
Medium Diameter 1000-hr Woody Fuel Tons per acre Biomass of medium diameter 1000-hr woody fuel (6 to 12 in; 15.24 to 30.48 cm)
Large Diameter 1000-hr Woody Fuel Tons per acre Biomass of large diameter 1000-hr woody fuel (>12 in; > 30.48 cm).
Litter Tons per acre Biomass of the litter layer.
Duff Tons per acre Biomass of the duff layer.

Potential Fire Behavior

     
Parameter Unit Description
Flame Length – Moderate Feet The potential surface fire flame length under moderate conditions. This flame length does not take any crown fire activity into account.
Flame Length – Severe Feet The potential surface fire flame length under severe conditions. This flame length does not take any crown fire activity into account.
Total Flame Length – Moderate Feet The potential total flame length under moderate conditions. This flame length does not take any crown fire activity into account.
Total Flame Length – Severe Feet The potential total flame length under severe conditions. This flame length does not take any crown fire activity into account.
Type of Fire – Moderate NA Fire Type; surface, passive, active crown or conditional crown under moderate conditions.
Type of Fire – Severe NA Fire Type; surface, passive, active crown or conditional crown under severe conditions.
Probability of Torching – Moderate NA The potential probability of torching under moderate conditions, based on surface fire intensity.
Probability of Torching – Severe NA The potential probability of torching under severe conditions, based on surface fire intensity.
20-ft Wind Speed Torching Index – Severe Miles per hour The 20-ft wind speed required to cause torching of some trees under severe conditions.
20-ft Wind Speed Crown Index – Severe Miles per hour The 20-ft wind speed required to cause an active crown fire under severe conditions.

Potential Fire Effects

Parameter Unit Description
Basal Area Killed – Moderate Percent Potential tree mortality measured by the percent of basal area killed under moderate conditions.
Basal Area Killed – Severe Percent Potential tree mortality measured by the percent of basal area killed under severe conditions.
Total Volume Killed – Moderate Feet3 Potential tree mortality measured by the total volume killed under moderate conditions.
Total Volume Killed – Severe Feet3 Potential tree mortality measured by the total volume killed under severe conditions.
Smoke Emissions PM2.5 – Moderate Tons per acre The potential amount of smoke emissions (PM2.5) under moderate conditions.
Smoke Emissions PM2.5 – Severe Tons per acre The potential amount of smoke emissions (PM2.5) under severe conditions.

Fuel Models and Weights Crosswalk

Parameter Unit Description
Primary Fuel Model NA The primary fire behavior fuel model that best represents the stand.
Primary Fuel Model Weight Percent The percent weighting for the primary fuel model.
Secondary Fuel Model NA The secondary fire behavior fuel model that best represents the stand.
Secondary Fuel Model Weight Percent The percent weighting for the secondary fuel model.
