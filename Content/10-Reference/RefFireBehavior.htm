Fire Behavior Modules

IFTDSS supports the following fire behavior modules:

  • crown fire behavior (as implemented in BehavePlus)
  • fire behavior (as implemented in FlamMap), both across a landscape and for individual stands
  • surface fire behavior (as implemented in BehavePlus)
  • surface fire behavior (FCCS)
  • surface fire potentials (FCCS)
  • surface fire behavior, size, and spread (as implemented in BehavePlus)

Fire Behavior Fuel Models

Mathematical fire behavior models (i.e., Rothermel, 1972) require descriptions of fuel properties as input parameters, which are then used with the fire behavior modules to calculate fire behavior potentials. These collections of fuel properties have become known as fire behavior fuel models.

Fire behavior fuel models are classified into four groups—grasses, brush, timber, and slash. The differences in fire behavior among these groups are related to the fuel load and its distribution among the fuel particle size classes (Anderson, 1982).

The Fire Behavior Fuel Models section contains more information on the fuel models themselves and on related information, such as moisture content and curing.
