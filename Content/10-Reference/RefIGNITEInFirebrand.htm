Input Parameters – Probability of Ignition from Firebrands

Name Units Brief Description
1-hr Fuel Moisture Percentage The portion of the 1-hour timelag dead fuel that is water, calculated on a dry weight basis. This value is also used for dead herbaceous fuel moisture content for dynamic fuel models.
Air Temperature Degrees F, degrees C The ambient dry bulb temperature measured in the shade. This value is used to calculate scorch height and probability of ignition from a firebrand.
Fuel Shading from the Sun Percentage The portion of the fuel that is not receiving direct sunlight due to cloud cover and overstory shading. This value is used to calculate the probability of ignition by a firebrand. Shading that is less than 50% is sometimes referred to as being in an unshaded condition; 50% or more shading is referred to as a shaded condition.
