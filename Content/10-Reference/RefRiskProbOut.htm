Output Parameters

Name Units Brief Description
Burn Probability at Low Flame Lengths Percentage The likelihood that if the area represented by a pixel burns, it will burn within the user-defined low flame length class.
Burn Probability at Medium Flame Lengths Percentage The likelihood that if the area represented by a pixel burns, it will burn within the user-defined medium flame length class.
Burn Probability at High Flame Lengths Percentage The likelihood that if the area represented by a pixel burns, it will burn within the user-defined high flame length class.
Burn Probability at Very High Flame Lengths Percentage The likelihood that if the area represented by a pixel burns, it will burn within the user-defined very high flame length class.
Overall Burn Probability Percentage The likelihood that the area represented by a pixel will burn given a random ignition across the area of interest (for a specific set of environmental conditions). Burn probability is not an estimate of the future likelihood of a wildfire and should not be confused with empirical wildfire occurrence probabilities.
Relative Net Value Change (Default) NA The relative net change in land value due to fire is assumed to be a function of fire behavior, and includes both positive and negative effects of fire.

Net Value Change (NVC) = ∑∑ (NVC multiplier at x flame length * burn probability at x flame length), where x is the user-defined flame length class.

By default, the NVC output value range is binned by 20% for each of five categories.

  • Greatest Loss/Least Benefit
  • High Loss/Low Benefit
  • Average Loss/Benefit
  • Low Loss/High Benefit
  • Least Loss/Greatest Benefit

See Relative Net Value Change for more information.
