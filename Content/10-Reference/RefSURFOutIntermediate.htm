Surface Fire Behavior Intermediate Outputs

You can select one or more of these options.

Name Units Brief Description
Bulk Density Pounds per feet3, kilograms per meter2 The total oven-dry fuel loading per cubic volume of fuel bed (fuel loading divided by fuel bed depth). This is an intermediate value in Rothermel's surface fire rate of spread (ROS) model (1972).
Characteristic dead fuel moisture Percentage The calculated dead fuel moisture for the whole fuel bed. The calculation involves weighting by dead fuel loading and SA/V ratio for each dead class.
Characteristic live fuel moisture Percentage The calculated live fuel moisture for the whole fuel bed. The calculation involves weighting by live fuel loading and SA/V ratio for each live class.
Characteristic SA/V Feet2 per feet3, meters2 per meter3 The fuel bed characteristic SA/V ratio (sigma) is a weighted value for all dead and live fuel classes. This is an intermediate value in Rothermel's surface fire ROS model (1972).
Dead fuel reaction intensity Btus per feet2 per minute, kilowatts per meter2 The rate of the energy release per area (square foot or square meter) within the flaming front generated by the dead fuel. Reaction intensity is not affected by wind, slope, or direction of spread. Reaction intensity is a sum of the dead and the live fuel reaction intensity.
Flame residence time Minutes A function of the characteristic SA/V ratio of the fuel bed as calculated by Rothermel's surface fire ROS model (1972). Flame residence time is used to calculate flame depth and heat per unit area. Flame residence time only applies to the fine fuels that carry fire spread. (Anderson, 1969) Fireline intensity is calculated as the product of reaction intensity times flame depth or heat per unit area times surface ROS.
Heat sink Btus per feet3, kilojoules per meter3 This parameter is used in computing the surface ROS. ROS is calculated from the ratio of the heat flux received from the source (heat source, which is the numerator) and the heat required for ignition by the potential fuel (heat sink, the denominator). The heat required for ignition is dependent upon ignition temperature, moisture content of the fuel, and the amount of fuel involved in the ignition process. This is an intermediate value in Rothermel's surface fire spread model (1972).
Heat source Btus per feet2 per minute, kilowatts per meter2 This parameter is used in computing the surface ROS. ROS is calculated from the ratio of the heat flux received from the source (heat source, which is the numerator) and the heat required for ignition by the potential fuel (heat sink, which is the denominator). The propagating heat flux is increased by wind and slope factors due to flame tilting over the potential fuel to calculate the total propagating heat flux or heat source. This is an intermediate value in Rothermel's surface fire ROS model (1972).
Live fuel moisture of extinction Percentage The characteristic moisture content of the live fuel above which the live fuel is a heat sink. Below that value, the live fuel is a heat source and contributes to the surface fire spread. Live fuel moisture of extinction is calculated from dead fuel moisture, live fuel moisture, load, size, and dead fuel moisture of extinction.
Live fuel reaction intensity Btus per feet2 per minute, kilowatts per meter2 The rate of the energy release per area (square foot or square meter) within the flaming front generated by the live fuel. Reaction intensity is not affected by wind, slope, or direction of spread. Reaction intensity is a sum of the dead and the live fuel reaction intensity.
Packing Ratio A ratio The packing ratio (Beta) is the ratio of the fuel bed bulk density (lb / ft3) to the fuel particle density (lb / ft3). This is an intermediate value in Rothermel's surface fire ROS model (1972).
Relative Packing Ratio A ratio The ratio of the fuel bed packing ratio to the optimum packing ratio (Beta divided by Beta-op). This ratio is a factor in determining the optimum reaction velocity. This is an intermediate value in Rothermel's surface fire ROS model (1972).
Slope factor NA An intermediate value in Rothermel's surface fire ROS model (1972). Slope factor is used to increase the surface rate of spread. ROS is calculated for zero wind and zero slope (R0) and then increased by the wind factor (phi-w) and the slope factor (phi-s).

ROS = R0(1 + phi-w + phi-s)
Wind factor NA An intermediate value in Rothermel's surface fire ROS model (1972). Wind factor is used to increase the surface rate of spread. ROS is calculated for zero wind and zero slope (R0) and then increased by the wind factor (phi-w) and the slope factor (phi-s).

ROS = R0(1 + phi-w + phi-s)
