Response Functions and Values at Risk

In the risk pathways, you define one or more values at risk, then assign a response function to each value at risk.

The following table provides some examples of values at risk and the response functions assigned to them.

Value at Risk Response Function Description
Airport 7 Moderate increasing loss, from mild loss at low intensity to a strong loss at very high intensity.
Archeological Site 11 Moderate loss from fire at all fire intensities.
Oak Woodlands 4 Moderate benefit at low fire intensities to moderate loss at high fire intensities.
Wetlands 2 All fire is beneficial; moderate benefit at low fire intensity and mild benefit at higher intensity.
Wildland/Urban Interface 12 Strong loss from fire at all fire intensities.
