Fire Emission Production Simulator (FEPS)

The Fire Emission Production Simulator (FEPS) predicts hourly consumption, emissions, and heat release of wildland fires. Total consumption values from Consume or IFT-FOFEM are distributed over the life of the burn to generate hourly emission and release information. FEPS can be used for most forest, shrub and grassland types in North America.

Originally developed as a revision to the Emission Production Model (EPM), FEPS version 1.0 was renamed due to extensive changes in data requirements and algorithms. EPM was a Fortran-based model that predicted emission source strength for prescribed burning (Sandberg and Peterson, 1984).

FEPS was recently recoded to work as a calculator module within the BlueSky modeling framework (http://playground.firesmoke.us) and IFTDSS. FEPS is used extensively in the southeastern United States to generate intermediate values used in VSmoke plume predictions (http://webcam.srs.fs.fed.us/tools/vsmoke).
