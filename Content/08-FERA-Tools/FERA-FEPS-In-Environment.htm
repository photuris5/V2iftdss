Input Parameters—Environmental

The environmental inputs in the following table are required for each FEPS run. These parameters represent a 24-hour period. Once you enter values for these parameters, those values are used in the weather model to populate the hourly meteorological data. You can modify the hourly meteorological data values.

Name Units Brief Description

DAYLIGHT DATA
Sunrise Time Hours The hour of sunrise. The hours are displayed in military time; for example, 1 p.m. will be displayed as hour 13.
Midday Time Hours The hour when the sun is highest above the horizon (displayed in military time).
Sunset Time Hours The hour of sunset (displayed inmilitary time).

TEMPERATURE AND RELATIVE HUMIDITY DATA
Air Temperature - Maximum Fahrenheit, Celsius The maximum air temperature throughout the duration of the fire.  Air temperature is the ambient dry bulb temperature measured in the shade.
Air Temperature - Minimum Fahrenheit, Celsius The minimum air temperature throughout the duration of the fire.  Air temperature is the ambient dry bulb temperature measured in the shade.
Relative Humidity - Maximum Percent The maximum relative humidity throughout the duration of the fire.  Relative humidity is the ratio of the amount of moisture in the air compared to the amount the air could hold if fully saturated (100%).
Relative Humidity - Minimum Percent The minimum relative humidity throughout the duration of the fire.  Relative humidity is the ratio of the amount of moisture in the air compared to the amount the air could hold if fully saturated (100%).

WIND SPEED DATA
Midflame Wind Speed - Maximum Miles/hour, kilometers/hour The maximum wind speed at midflame height throughout the duration of the fire.
Midflame Wind Speed - Minimum Miles/hour, kilometers/hour The minimum wind speed at midflame height throughout the duration of the fire.
Transport Wind Speed - Maximum Miles/hour, kilometers/hour The maximum wind speed within the mixing layer. Generally refers to the maximum rate at which emissions will be transported from one area to another. Usually, a wind under 8 mi/h (under 12.9 km/h) restricts an agency from burning.
Transport Wind Speed - Minimum Miles/hour, kilometers/hour The minimum wind speed within the mixing layer. Generally refers to the minimum rate at which emissions will be transported from one area to another. Usually, a wind under 8 mi/h (under 12.9 km/h) restricts an agency from burning.
