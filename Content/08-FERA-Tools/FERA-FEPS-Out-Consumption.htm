Output Parameters—Hourly Consumption and Emissions Data

When you export these data, they are saved in a file in CSV format. For information on what each parameter means in the exported file, see Exported Hourly Consumption and Emissions Parameters.

Fire Size and Fuel Consumption

Total Consumption Tons, megagrams The cumulative amount of fuel consumed during the fire.

Hourly Buoyancy Results
Briggs Buoyancy Feet4 per second3, Meters4 per second3 Same as buoyancy flux; relates to the buoyancy of the plume at the origin of the fire.
Briggs Maximum Heat Rise Feet, Meters The final effective plume height above origin of the fire based on Briggs buoyancy; models plume rise based on a smokestack model.
Briggs Stability Feet/second, Meters/second Stability coefficient.  Stability determines whether the buoyancy of a plume increases or decreases with plume rise.
Buoyancy Efficiency NA The portion of heat release from a fire that produces buoyancy, BE.
Combustion Efficiency NA An intermediate parameter used to calculate emission rates and heat release.
Drift PM2.5 Tons, megagrams The PM2.5 drift/smoke emissions at a given hour.
Entrainment  Efficiency NA The portion of smoke production entrained in the convection column.
Lofted PM2.5 Tons, megagrams The PM2.5 lofted emissions at a given hour.
Maximum Plume Height Feet, meters The hourly elevation at the top of the plume using empirical equations.
Minimum Plume Height Feet, meters The hourly elevation at the bottom of the plume using empirical equations.
Plume Heat Release British thermal units, kilojoules Heat release of the plume at a given hour.
