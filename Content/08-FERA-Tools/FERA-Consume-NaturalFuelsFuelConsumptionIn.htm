Input Parameters – Fuel Consumption for Natural Fuels

Name Units Brief Description

Inputs
1000-hr Woody Fuel Moisture Percentage Moisture content of 1000-hour (> 3 inch; > 7.62 cm) dead fuel. Variable 1000-hr woody fuel moisture is available for this pathway.
Duff Fuel Moisture Percentage Moisture content of duff. If your fuelbed does not have a duff layer, or you cannot measure duff fuel moisture, use the following to select the most representative fuel moisture category: Low = 30%, Medium = 70%, High = 120%.
Percent Canopy Loading Consumed Percentage Percentage of canopy loading consumed by fire. If you do not know the percent of canopy consumed, use the following guidelines: Prescribed burn = 0%, Wildfire = 60%.
Percent of Shrub Blackened by Fire Percentage Estimated percentage of the shrub stratum that is blackened by fire.

Selector
Consumption Equation Drop-down list Select a consumption equation that best represents your geographic location and vegetation type. The natural consumption equations are based on three regional fuel consumption studies and include: (1) Western (ponderosa pine data set), (2) Southern (long-needled pine data set), and (3) Boreal (deep forest floor consumption).
