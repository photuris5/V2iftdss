Welcome to IFTDSS

Welcome to the Interagency Fuels Treatment Decision Support System (IFTDSS). IFTDSS is a web-based model and data integration framework that organizes previously existing and newly developed fire and fuels software applications to make fuels treatment planning and analysis more efficient and effective. IFTDSS has been developed forfuels treatment specialists by fuels treatment specialists.

Introduction to IFTDSS: 

Inception

In 2007, the NWCG and many others presented the JFSP with a persistent problem: namely, that the proliferation of unconnected, partially redundant, and often unverified models and software systems for fire and fuels management was causing confusion and inefficiencies. Hundreds of standalone software systems, each with unique user interfaces, require a substantial amount of time to learn. Furthermore, the amount of time spent on data transformations among models is often greater than the amount of time spent on analysis.

IFTDSS is a result of the Software Tools and Systems study conducted by the JFSP to look into these issues. All project planning and accomplishment documents are published at www.frames.gov/ift-dss.

For a brief, readable overview of IFTDSS, please see A Powerful New Planning Environment for Fuels Managers: the Interagency Fuels Treatment Decision Support System in the December 2009 issue of Fire Science Digest.

Benefits

Because IFTDSS provides a single portal for access to several sources of data and models for fuels treatment planning, it conveys a number of benefits and solutions. Some of the highlights of IFTDSS are that it

  • reduces or eliminates the amount of time a user needs to spend learning new interfaces and transforming data.
  • provides workflows that organize the business needs of fuels treatment planners.
  • makes it easy for planners to efficiently reuse and share their work products.
  • provides a consistent analysis framework for all users.

Status

As of October 2012, the current version of IFTDSS is version 2.0. This version is available for testing and evaluation by members of the various cooperating stakeholder groups.

Extensive guidance by a broad range of fuels treatment specialists over the last three years has resulted in the identification of five critically important workflow processes:

  1. Prescribed burn planning and burn plan development;
  2. Wildfire hazard analysis across a landscape of interest;
  3. Landscape wildfire risk assessments for current conditions;
  4. Landscape wildfire risk assessment for simulated and treated future landscape conditions; and
  5. Assessing fuels treatment effectiveness over time.

Providing Feedback

If you have any issues using the system or if you have suggestions for improvements, please submit your feedback to the IFTDSS development team using the IFTDSS feedback link in the upper right corner of any screen.
